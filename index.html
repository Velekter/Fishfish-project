Brown crab

To catch brown crabs, you need nothing more than a flashlight and quick reflexes—the last thing brown crabs see is a flash and a striking hand. But you must act quickly: in a second or two, the crab realizes what's happening and uses its claws to pinch you as hard as it can, and it won’t let go easily. Thus, crab fishing not only provides delicious food but also gives an adrenaline rush.

Ideal conditions for fish.

Gulf Stream, carrying warm water from the Gulf of Mexico across the Atlantic Ocean, flows north along the Norwegian coast into the clear, pure icy waters of the Arctic. This creates ideal conditions for an incredibly rich marine ecosystem.

Small fish, large fish, mollusks, and other forms of life. Some of them are locals, while others migrate or pass through the globe. Some prefer the cold open sea, while others live in calm, deep fjords protected by a wall of islands and skerries. Each plays its role in the complex and fascinating marine life cycle.

The diversity of seafood from this environment is unique. This is one of the main reasons why Norway is the second largest exporter of seafood in the world. And there's also a reason why chefs and connoisseurs around the globe choose seafood from Norway: you'll find it difficult to find such selection and quality anywhere else.

The best quality seafood

Norwegian seafood is high-quality seafood, and we are proud to share it. This is why our seafood is enjoyed all over the world. The international significance of this industry is clearly demonstrated by the fact that Norwegian seafood travels to about 150 countries. We are the second largest exporter of seafood in the world.

Ecological Consumption

The magnificent coastal nature of Norway and the diversity of life it supports have been the foundation of our existence since the Stone Age. We know we owe it to future generations. The sea is a gift that keeps on giving, but only as long as we continue to respect it and treat what we have been given with care.

Ideal Conditions
farming

Norway has one of the most advanced and well-established systems for sustainable development and management in both fishing and aquaculture. This is not a coincidence, but rather systematic work at all levels to ensure consumer safety and the welfare of fish.

A strong emphasis on animal welfare and strict regulations mean that our seafood is consistently of high quality and safe for consumption. Production is environmentally friendly and responsible, leaving a minimal footprint on the environment.
